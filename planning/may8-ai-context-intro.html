For the first time, we can measure it

Most AI-and-jobs predictions rely on hypothetical capability. Anthropic's 2025 study measured actual usage — what people really use Claude for at work, task by task.

Old approach — theoretical exposure

"Could AI do this task in principle?" → assign a score per occupation

Result: almost every office job looks 90%+ exposed

New approach — observed exposure

Match millions of real Claude conversations to BLS occupation task lists

Result: actual adoption is far lower — and very uneven

Automation is weighted more than augmentation — a task where AI fully replaces human effort counts more than one where it just assists.

The implication for this room: Applied physicists — writing grants, analysing data, teaching, reviewing literature — are firmly in the high-exposure category. Not because AI replaces physical experiments, but because the cognitive and communication tasks surrounding them are highly automatable.

This is not a crisis — it's a capability shift. The question is whether you use it or let others use it first.

We are early — the gap is still open

94% theoretical capability. 33% actual observed adoption. That gap closes over time.

Nov 2022
ChatGPT launches — public inflection point. Hiring into exposed roles begins slowing.
2023–24
Claude, GPT-4, Gemini — frontier capability expands. Early adopters build significant advantages.
2025
Anthropic study: adoption still only 33% of theoretical potential. Most organisations have barely started.
2026–?
Legal, software, and organisational barriers erode. Coverage grows. Today's gap narrows.

But what does responsible use look like?

Karl de Fine Licht (Chalmers, Ethics & Technology) identifies that AI is entering every phase of research — and the effects on quality are real.

GenAI is used across the full research cycle

① Idea generation   ② Research design
③ Data collection   ④ Analysis
⑤ Writing

Observed effect when AI is used: lower quality · less original · less time invested

Four values that define research

Downstream — Societal benefit ("curing cancer")
Epistemic — New knowledge, robust findings
Practice — Curiosity, judgment, critical discussion
Civic-pedagogical — Better teachers, public engagers

The answer is not "prohibit" or "allow freely." It is: allow some use · hold ourselves accountable · revise institutional norms · hold others accountable.

de Fine Licht · Chalmers · "Responsible Research in the Age of GenAI" · 2026

What this means for today

The disruption is structural — labor market signals are already visible, especially for the next generation entering research careers.
The risks are real — AI use correlates with lower quality, less originality, less time invested. Your domain expertise is the safeguard, not the model.
The window is open — 94% theoretical capability, 33% actual adoption. Early, critical adopters gain durable advantages.
The goal today — not to hand over your research to AI, but to understand what it can and cannot do, and to start building your own informed practice.
