The shift our students will be hired for

An agent on its own is already doing the work we set as homework. A student on their own is no longer the comparison point. The only piece our graduates can sell on the 2027 job market is the combination.

+
Student + AI
>
AI alone
or
Student alone
The differentiator is not "can you use AI". It is whether the student can produce something the agent could not have produced without them. That is what we should design our courses to develop.

Proposing an agentic-AI course for 2027

SKxxxx • code TBD 7.5 hp + 2.0 hp PhD workshop Master's elective HT 2027
Agentic AI for Science and Engineering
Agentisk AI för vetenskap och teknik
Replaces SK2538 / FSK3538.
The central bet. The durable graduate skill of the AI-agent era is not writing more code — agents produce code essentially for free. It is collaborating with AI agents to produce work that neither human nor agent can produce alone. The course is built around that one thesis end to end.

Course shape

  • Cross-track elective — designed for all five Engineering Physics tracks.
  • Studio format — weekly hands-on labs, one continuous agent build across the term.
  • Capstone — public showcase + live oral defence with AI-augmented panel.

Six rotating modules

  • Imaging & CV · Molecular structure · Single-cell & genomics
  • Precision medicine · Rotating physics topic (quantum / photonics / materials, year by year) · Autonomous discovery & multi-agent systems

Four skills — the spine of the course

When software implementation cost goes to zero, the durable graduate skills are these four. They are the course's intended learning outcomes.

Communicate

Write specs, decompose problems, articulate constraints & success criteria, iterate through dialog with the agent.

Build

Design and ship multi-tool / multi-agent workflows for real users. Decide what to delegate, what to wrap as deterministic tools, what to keep in the loop.

Verify

Failure-mode taxonomies. Evaluation harnesses. Adversarial probes. Calibrate trust case by case — catch hallucination and silent failure before they propagate.

Use responsibly

Disclose AI use through your own conversation history. Navigate ethics. Produce reproducible, FAIR, openly-licensed artefacts.

Each skill is examined in all three exam modules — not in one place each. That's the cross-module triangulation that keeps every ILO honest.

Three exam modules — one recurring artefact

LAB1 2.5 hp • P/F
Build & peer chat review

Weekly studio labs. Each week the student exports their AI chat history; a paired peer reviews against a published rubric. An LLM pre-validates each peer review; disagreements flag to the TA.

SEM1 1.5 hp • P/F
Oral practice & presentation

Weekly breakout discussions. Everyone prepares; 1–2 students drawn at random to present (case-method cold-calling, Christensen tradition). Two formal presentation blocks per term.

PRO1 3.5 hp • A–F
Real agent system project

Real external-user mandate — own contact or the Guest-Lecturer Project Menu (1–3 open problems per guest). 12-min public defence, AI-augmented panel, 5-min Failure Report. Agent Auditor grades autonomously; students design to be agent-gradable.

The single recurring written artefact across all three modules is the student's own AI conversation history. Disclosed, peer-reviewed, archived. It cannot be post-rationalised — honest use of AI becomes the graded skill.
Scalability target. Teacher load drops from ~22 h/wk on current SK2538 → ~6 h/wk on HT 2027 (~70% reduction). The reduction comes from LLM pre-validation, the Agent Auditor, and cohort scaffolding — the same operating model the course teaches students to build.

Why this design — and not the standard format

Four deliberate departures from the SCI default. Each one anchors a graduate skill we cannot afford to leave assumed.

Examination

The defence is the exam.

Public showcase. AI-augmented examiner panel that probes the work live, on the spot. A 5-minute Failure Report that inverts demo-day theatre.

Why → mirrors how every reviewer in 2027 will work — AI-augmented, on real artefacts, in public. Not a closed-room oral.
Artefact

The chat history is the artefact.

Across LAB1, SEM1, PRO1. Exported, peer-reviewed weekly, archived. Paper deliverables minimised.

Why → the only evidence of how a student worked with an agent that cannot be post-rationalised. Honest use becomes the graded skill.
Project

The project serves a real external user.

Research supervisor, partner-lab scientist, partner-company engineer, or open problem from the Guest-Lecturer Project Menu. Witness statement + concrete utility example. A course Agent Auditor grades autonomously.

Why → real-world utility is the binding constraint; the witness cannot be gamed; the Agent Auditor scales the rigour without scaling the teacher.
Verification

Verification is an ILO, not an afterthought.

Failure-mode taxonomies, eval harnesses, adversarial probes — designed and submitted as graded work, not bolted on at the end.

Why → AI outputs lie confidently. Recognising failure modes — and building the harness that catches them — is what graduates will be hired for.
