## Karpathy material (Wei, 7 May)

> Andrej Karpathy said he's never felt more behind as a programmer.
> Let that sink in for a second.

Specific points from his recent talk:

- **December 2025 was the real turning point.** Not gradual — a step change where agentic workflows just suddenly worked reliably. A lot of people missed it.
- **MenuGen story.** He built a whole app to show photos of restaurant menu items. Then saw someone solve the same problem with one prompt to a multimodal AI. His own words: "my app shouldn't exist."
- **Vibe coding ≠ agentic engineering.** Vibe coding raises the floor for everyone (anyone can produce something). Agentic engineering is how professionals go faster *without dropping the quality bar*. Very different things.
- **Jagged intelligence.** The same model that refactors a 100k-line codebase will tell you to walk 50 m to a car wash. Still can't figure out you have to drive there.
- **The line that matters most**, told to Karpathy by someone else:
  > "You can outsource your thinking, but you cannot outsource your understanding."

These appear in the intro deck as slides 4, 5, 6 and 9 (the takeaway slide).
