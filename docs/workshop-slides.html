Background: How We Got Here

The Engineering Mechanics Retreat (3 days)

  • Day 1: ML foundations — SVD, regression, neural nets
  • Day 2: CNNs, RNNs, Transformers
  • Day 3: External AI coach — LLMs, coding assistants, demos

Success factors: Domain-relevant examples, good & bad AI examples, external expertise

Feb 18 Handover Meeting (Wei + Jonas + Fredrik + Artem + Marco)

  • APHYS faculty have strong math — less need for derivations
  • Biggest gap: ML theory vs. practical AI tool usage
  • AI agents have changed everything since that retreat
  • The urgent crisis: impact on teaching and learning
Key consensus: Adapt, don't copy. Shift from theory-heavy to agent-first. 2 days, not 3. Address the teaching crisis head-on.

Two Perspectives, One Workshop

Day 1 — AI Agent Onboarding: Personalized AI tutor for concepts (morning). Build real things — train models, create apps, automate workflows (afternoon).
Day 2 — Impact & Action: Solve your own research/productivity problem with AI (morning). Teaching crisis, ethics, assignment redesign, action planning (afternoon).
Design philosophy: Not a coding workshop. Not a lecture series. AI agents are the interface for everything. Faculty focus on understanding, judgment, and building — not typing code.

Day 1 Morning: The AI Tutor Prompt

Each participant gets a personalized AI tutor — adapted to their research area, level, and interests.

How It Works

  • Participants receive a crafted instruction prompt
  • They provide their background: research area, ML familiarity, programming level
  • The AI generates tailored training materials covering:
SVD, PCA, regression → Neural networks → CNNs, RNNs → Transformers → AI agents & LLMs
  • All examples grounded in their specific physics domain
  • Self-paced: 2 hours of interactive exploration

Why This Is Better Than Lectures

Personalized: A spectroscopist learns through spectroscopy. A biophysicist through microscopy.

Adaptive: Experts get advanced material. Beginners get patient guidance. No one bored, no one lost.

Meta-learning: They learn ABOUT AI BY USING AI. The medium teaches the message.

Interactive: Ask questions, get exercises, explore tangents — impossible in a lecture hall.

Day 2 Morning: AI for Your Real Work

What wastes the most of your time? What tasks do you dread? AI can probably help.

For Researchers

  • Analyze real experimental data from your lab
  • Build a model for a current research question
  • Create a literature review on your topic
  • Prototype a new analysis method

For Educators

  • Review student exams with AI-assisted feedback
  • Generate suggestions on student reports
  • Create course materials and exercise templates
  • Redesign a grading or feedback workflow

For Daily Productivity

  • Organize and summarize meeting notes
  • Draft grant sections, committee reports, emails
  • Create financial plans and budget estimates
  • Build personal tools for recurring tasks
  • Automate any repetitive workflow
2 hours of focused work on YOUR real problem — not a prepared exercise. Facilitators help you frame the task for AI and evaluate the results.
Show & Tell: Volunteers share what they accomplished. Group discusses: what should we always use AI for? Where to be cautious?

Day 2 Afternoon: The Uncomfortable Reality

The Assignment Demolition

An AI agent completes a real APHYS course assignment — live, in front of everyone. Code, analysis, figures, written explanation. It would earn a good grade.

Then we discuss:

  • What were we actually testing with that assignment?
  • Can we tell AI output from a real student's work?
  • If a student uses AI and spends 30 min understanding — vs. 10 hours doing it manually — who learned more?
The question is not "how do we stop students from using AI?" The question is "what do we want students to learn, and how do we know they learned it?"

The Bigger Picture

  • Junior career squeeze: Companies use AI instead of hiring juniors
  • Entry-level roles in coding, analysis, writing — shrinking
  • The learn → junior role → expertise path is breaking
  • Young people losing the chance to learn through doing

Ethical Questions

  • What do we owe students about AI preparedness?
  • How do we prevent AI from widening inequality?
  • What is still uniquely human — and how do we cultivate it?
  • Should AI literacy be mandatory in physics education?

Rethinking Teaching: The Workshop Exercise

Human + AI > AI alone > Human alone — but only if the human brings real expertise.

Deep Understanding

Know why, not just what. First-principles reasoning.

Critical Evaluation

Spot errors AI misses. Physics judgment over computation.

Problem Framing

AI answers. Only humans know what to ask.
