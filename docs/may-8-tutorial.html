Step-by-step guide for the hands-on project session · 16:00

Exercise 1 — ChatGPT hands-on (13:45, 15 min)

Open chat.openai.com in your browser. Work in groups of 2–4. Paste the prompt below and replace the placeholders with your own abstracts — or use the three provided at the bottom of this section.

Here are 3 abstracts from my research field: [ABSTRACT 1 — paste here, or use one from the samples below] [ABSTRACT 2 — paste here] [ABSTRACT 3 — paste here] Please: 1. Identify the main shared theme across these papers (2 sentences) 2. Point out one contradiction or unresolved tension between them 3. Suggest one research question none of them has answered yet 4. Draft a 100-word introduction paragraph for a study that addresses that gap — for a specialist reader in my field

Then iterate — try one of these follow-ups:

  • "Make the gap paragraph more urgent — emphasise why the question matters now."
  • "What are you most uncertain about in your answer? Where might you be wrong?"
  • "Argue against the research question you just suggested."
  • "Rewrite the paragraph for a VR grant panel — non-specialist audience."
Sample abstracts (Applied Physics) — click to expand

Abstract 1 — Ultrafast X-ray spectroscopy:
We report time-resolved X-ray absorption spectroscopy measurements of charge-transfer dynamics in a cobalt-based molecular catalyst at a free-electron laser. Using a 50-fs X-ray pulse, we resolve a metal-to-ligand charge transfer state that persists for 3.2 ps before relaxing to the ground state. Our findings suggest that the lifetime of this state is strongly dependent on solvent polarity, with implications for the design of more efficient artificial photosynthesis systems.

Abstract 2 — Ultrafast optical spectroscopy:
Using transient absorption spectroscopy with 10-fs time resolution, we track excited-state dynamics in a series of ruthenium photosensitisers. We observe a branching between intersystem crossing and internal conversion channels that depends sensitively on the ligand field strength. Contrary to earlier reports, our data indicate that triplet state formation occurs on a sub-100-fs timescale, faster than vibrational cooling. These results challenge existing mechanistic models and have direct implications for solar energy conversion materials.

Abstract 3 — Computational chemistry:
We present density functional theory calculations of excited-state potential energy surfaces for a family of transition metal complexes used as photocatalysts. Our calculations reveal a previously unreported conical intersection between the singlet and triplet manifolds at a metal–ligand bond elongation of 0.15 Å. This intersection provides a barrierless pathway for ultrafast intersystem crossing, consistent with recent experimental observations. We propose that tuning the ligand field to shift this intersection can rationally optimise charge-transfer yields.

Open your terminal and navigate to a folder with data, code, or any project files. Then start Claude Code. If you have no project handy, use the desktop — Claude can work with anything.

Advanced hands-on — mini-project (15:45, 45 min)

Work in groups. Use the brainstorming prompt below to define a small project you can actually build in 30 minutes. Aim small — something that works beats something ambitious that doesn't.

Step 1 — Brainstorm with AI

Paste this into ChatGPT or Claude Code:

I am attending an AI tutorial at KTH Applied Physics. I have 30 minutes to build a small project with AI tools. My research/teaching area: [describe in 1–2 sentences] Tools available: ChatGPT, Claude Code, Python Data I have with me (if any): [describe or say "none"] Please ask me 3 questions to help me find a good mini-project that is: realistic in 30 min, useful for my actual work, and lets me practice what I've learned today. After I answer, suggest 3 concrete project options with a specific first step for each.

Step 2 — Build it

Work with Claude Code or ChatGPT to build the project. When you get stuck, describe the problem in plain language — don't try to write the solution yourself first.

Step 3 — Prepare to share (optional)

If you'd like to present at the wrap-up, ask Claude Code to create a 2-slide summary:

Create a 2-slide summary of what I just built: Slide 1: What it does and why it's useful for my work Slide 2: What worked, what surprised me, and what I'd do next Make it as a simple HTML file I can open in my browser.

Step 4 — Submit to the gallery (optional)

If you built something useful, share it with the department. Run this in Claude Code:

Submit my project to the APHYS AI use-case gallery. Gallery API: https://hypha.aicell.io/kth-sci/artifacts/aphys-ai-gallery Tags to include: ["may8-tutorial", "research"] (or teaching/productivity) My project: Name: [short title] Description: [2–3 sentences what it does and who it helps] Tool: Claude Code (or ChatGPT) Difficulty: Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced Time saved estimate: [e.g. "~1 hour per analysis"]
