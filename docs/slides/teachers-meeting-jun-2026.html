The agent is powerful. Our graduates must be more powerful still.

What Jonas just showed has a durable lesson. The agent on its own is already doing work we used to set as homework. A student on their own falls behind it. A student who has practised the combination — that is the only piece our graduates can sell on the 2027 job market.

Student + AI > AI alone > Student without AI
The differentiator is not “can you use AI”. It is — can you produce something the agent could not have produced without you. That is what we should be testing for.

The line worth quoting

Andrej Karpathy — Sequoia AI Ascent, late 2025

You can outsource your thinking, but you cannot outsource your understanding. — Andrej Karpathy, late 2025
If anything I say today survives the meeting, I would like it to be this line. The whole question of what we teach — and what we examine for — sits inside it.
  • An agent can produce the work. The student who borrowed it does not own the result.
  • If we cannot tell the two apart in our assessments, we are not measuring learning — we are measuring access.

A course on the table — three design choices.

I am redesigning my course around this. 7.5 hp Master's elective + 2.0 hp PhD workshop, cross-track, in the SCI teaching committee now. The three load-bearing design choices — each one a direct attempt to graduate the left-hand side of the inequality:

The defence is the exam

The capstone is a real working agent system, defended at a public showcase open to industry — with a real-user witness statement and a peer reproducibility audit. The defence is not added to the exam. It is the exam.

The AI log is the artefact

The single recurring written deliverable across all three exam modules is the student's own AI conversation log. Disclosed, graded, archived. Honest use becomes a graded skill; hidden use stops being possible by construction.

You vs. you+AI vs. AI-alone

Every lab requires three submissions on the same task. Grading rewards the delta the student adds on top of the agent. The inequality from the previous slide becomes the graded exercise, week after week.

The course operates the way it teaches students to operate. The dominant activity of a course shapes the dominant skill the student leaves with — so the operating model itself uses agent-assisted grading, peer pair-review, and a public showcase as the exam.

What would you redesign?

SK2538 is in the SCI teaching committee for HT 2027. I would like one more course from this department — any track, any level — to put one assessment on the table along the same lines. Pick the course. Redesign one piece. Bring the result back here next year.

Open discussion. Which assessment in your own course is most exposed to the inequality on slide 2 — and what is the smallest change that would close that gap? Oral defence? AI-required with disclosure? Process portfolio? Re-derivation on the board? Something else?
