01 · the asymmetry that matters

The agent is powerful. The students we graduate must be more powerful still.

What Jonas just showed has a durable lesson. The agent on its own is already doing work we used to assign as homework. A student on their own falls behind it. A student who has practised the combination — that is the only piece our graduates can sell on the 2027 job market.

Student + AI > AI alone > Student without AI
The differentiator is not “can you use AI”. It is — “can you produce something the agent could not have produced without you”. That is what we should be testing for.

SK2538 / FSK3538  —  Agentic AI for Science and Engineering  ·  HT 2027

What I am redesigning my course around.

Three design choices, each one a direct attempt to graduate the left-hand side of the inequality. The course operates the way it teaches students to operate.

The defence is the exam

The capstone is a real working agent system. It is defended at a public showcase open to industry — with a real-user witness statement and a peer reproducibility audit. The defence is not added to the exam. It is the exam.

The AI log is the artefact

The single recurring written deliverable across all three exam modules is the student's own AI conversation log. Disclosed, graded, archived. Honest use becomes a graded skill; hidden use stops being possible by construction.

You vs. you+AI vs. AI-alone

Every lab requires three submissions on the same task. Grading rewards the delta the student adds on top of the agent. The inequality from slide 2 becomes the graded exercise, week after week.
