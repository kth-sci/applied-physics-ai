Where AI is in 2026 — and why this is not last year's conversation

Forget the 2022 ChatGPT story. The interesting timeline is the last 18 months.

2024

Chat with documents. Code completion. Hallucinations everywhere. Mostly toys for early adopters.

Mid 2025

Agents start working on multi-step tasks. Coding tools become useful. Still brittle, requires babysitting.

Late 2025 / 2026

Agentic workflows reliable enough to run unattended. Sub-agents coordinating. MCP, skills, memory across sessions.

If you opted out of paying attention 6 months ago, you are now significantly behind. That sentence is not hype — it is what people who do this for a living say about themselves.

The wake-up call — Andrej Karpathy, late 2025

Co-founder of OpenAI, ex-Tesla Director of AI, builds AI tools for a living

I have never felt more behind as a programmer. — Andrej Karpathy, late 2025

Two very different things — do not confuse them

Vibe coding

Anyone can build something rough by just telling the AI what they want. Raises the floor for everyone — a non-programmer ships a working tool over a weekend.

Great for prototypes, scripts, one-off helpers, exploration.

Agentic engineering

How professionals go faster without dropping the quality bar. Disciplined prompts, tested outputs, code review, rollback safety, version control.

Required when the work has to be correct, reproducible, or shipped to others.

For our research: vibe coding is fine for an exploratory analysis script. Anything that ends up in a paper or a shared codebase needs the engineering mindset on top.

Distinction popularized by Karpathy, late 2025

Jagged intelligence — the part nobody warns you about

The same model that can refactor a 100k-line codebase will tell you to walk 50 metres to a car wash to wash your car. Still cannot figure out you need to drive there. — paraphrasing Karpathy
  • Performance is wildly uneven across tasks — superhuman in one domain, naive in an adjacent one.
  • You cannot reason from "it nailed task A" to "it will nail task B."
  • Your physics intuition is exactly the missing piece — that is what catches the silly answers.
The skill that newly matters most is knowing which outputs to trust. Domain expertise is not obsolete — it is the bottleneck.

The one quote to take away

You can outsource your thinking, but you cannot outsource your understanding. — quoted by Karpathy from a conversation, late 2025

Use AI to remove drudgery. Do not use it to skip the part where you understand the problem.
