ChatGPT Quick-Start Guide

Master prompt engineering in 15 minutes. A one-page guide for Applied Physics faculty.

Step 1: Get Access (2 minutes)

  1. Go to chatgpt.com
  2. Click "Log in" and sign in with your KTH credentials (institutional SSO)
  3. You should have access to GPT-4o -- the most capable model. Verify by checking the model selector at the top of the chat.
  4. Start a new conversation and type your first question.
Tip: Use "New chat" for each distinct topic. ChatGPT remembers the conversation context within a chat -- keeping topics separate helps it give better answers.

Step 2: The Five Principles of Effective Prompting

These patterns will improve your results by an order of magnitude.

1. Be Specific -- State What You Want

Vague prompts give vague answers. Specify the task, the format, and the level of detail.

"Tell me about PCA."

Too vague -- you get a generic textbook answer.

"Explain PCA to a physicist who understands eigenvalue decomposition. Focus on how PCA relates to SVD, when to use it on spectroscopy data, and what the principal components physically represent. Keep it to 200 words."

Specific context, audience, scope, and format.

2. Give Context -- Tell It Who You Are

The more context you provide, the more tailored the response. Tell it your role, domain, and what you are trying to achieve.

"I am a faculty member in applied physics at KTH, specializing in ultrafast spectroscopy. I have a dataset of pump-probe measurements (time delay vs. differential absorption). I want to extract the decay time constants. Suggest three approaches, starting with the simplest, and explain when each is appropriate."

3. Specify the Output Format

Tell ChatGPT exactly how you want the answer structured.

"Compare supervised and unsupervised learning methods for spectral classification. Format your answer as a table with columns: Method, When to Use, Strengths, Weaknesses, Example in Physics."

Useful format requests: "as a bullet list", "as a table", "as Python code", "in 3 paragraphs", "as a step-by-step procedure", "as a LaTeX equation".

4. Iterate -- Refine the Output

Rarely is the first answer perfect. Treat it as a first draft and refine.

"Make it shorter and more technical."

"Add a concrete example using Raman spectroscopy data."

"The equation in point 3 is wrong -- the damping term should be exponential, not Gaussian. Fix it."

5. Assign a Role -- Frame the Expertise

Telling ChatGPT to adopt a specific role focuses its responses.

"You are an expert in optical spectroscopy and data analysis. A colleague asks you to review their Python script for fitting Lorentzian peaks to absorption spectra. Identify any issues with their fitting approach and suggest improvements."

The Prompt Formula

[Role] + [Context] + [Task] + [Format] + [Constraints]

Example: "You are an expert in XPS analysis [role]. I have survey scan data from a Ti thin film [context]. Identify all peaks and assign chemical states [task]. Present as a table [format]. Focus on the Ti 2p and O 1s regions [constraints]."

Power Phrases

  • "Think step by step" -- improves reasoning
  • "Be specific and concrete" -- reduces vagueness
  • "If you are unsure, say so" -- reduces hallucination
  • "Compare and contrast" -- structured analysis
  • "What am I missing?" -- catches blind spots
  • "Simplify this for a [audience]" -- adjusts level

Prompt Engineering Cheat Sheet

Quick patterns you can copy and adapt. Bookmark this section.

Explain a Concept

"Explain [concept] to someone with a background in [your field]. Use [physics analogy]. Keep it to [length]."

Example: "Explain attention mechanisms in transformers to a physicist familiar with Fourier analysis. Use the analogy of selective frequency filtering."

Analyze Data

"I have a dataset of [description]. The columns are [list]. I want to [goal]. Suggest an analysis approach and write Python code to implement it."

Example: "I have XPS spectra with columns: binding_energy, intensity. I want to identify and fit the peaks. Write Python code using scipy."

Draft Text

"Draft a [type of text] about [topic]. The audience is [who]. The tone should be [style]. Include [specific content]. Keep it to [length]."

Example: "Draft a 200-word abstract for a paper on using CNNs for cell morphology classification in fluorescence microscopy."

Debug Code

"Here is my Python code: [paste code]. I get this error: [paste error]. Explain what is wrong, fix it, and explain the fix."

Tip: Always paste the actual error message. ChatGPT is excellent at diagnosing stack traces.

Literature Review

"I am writing a review section on [topic]. Summarize the key approaches, their strengths and limitations, and identify open questions. Structure it as: Background, Methods, Gaps."

Warning: ChatGPT may generate plausible but fake references. Always verify citations independently.

Create Teaching Material

"Create [N] exam questions on [topic] for a [level] physics course. Include model answers. Difficulty: [easy/medium/hard]. Format: [multiple choice / open-ended / calculation]."

Example: "Create 5 exam questions on Fourier optics for a master's-level course. Mix conceptual and calculation questions."

Try It Yourself

Practice writing prompts. Type your attempt, then reveal an example of a well-crafted prompt.

Exercise 1: Explain a concept from your field

Scenario: A master's student asks you to explain Bragg diffraction. Write a prompt that would get ChatGPT to give a clear, physics-appropriate explanation.

"You are a physics professor. Explain Bragg diffraction to a master's student who understands wave mechanics but has not taken a crystallography course. Use the analogy of constructive interference in thin films. Include the Bragg equation and explain what each variable represents. Keep it to 150 words and include one concrete example with numerical values."

Note the elements: role, audience, analogy, specific content, format constraint.

Exercise 2: Get help with data analysis

Scenario: You have a CSV file with two columns (temperature and resistance) from a superconductivity experiment. You want to find the critical temperature. Write a prompt.

"I have a CSV file with columns: temperature_K and resistance_ohms, from a four-point probe measurement of a superconducting sample. I need to: (1) plot resistance vs temperature, (2) determine the critical temperature Tc defined as the midpoint of the resistive transition, (3) fit the transition region with a sigmoid function. Write Python code using pandas, numpy, scipy, and matplotlib. Add axis labels and a vertical line at Tc."

Specific about data format, analysis steps, definition of Tc, and visualization requirements.

Exercise 3: Draft a professional communication

Scenario: You need to write an email to a journal editor requesting an extension on a revision deadline. Write a prompt.

"Draft a polite, professional email to a journal editor (Physical Review Letters) requesting a 2-week extension on a manuscript revision deadline. The reason is that we need additional beam time at MAX IV to complete a requested control experiment. The tone should be respectful but confident. Keep it to 100 words. Sign it from 'Prof. [Name], KTH Royal Institute of Technology'."

Specific journal, concrete reason, defined tone, length constraint, and format.

Advanced Tips

For complex problems, add "Think step by step" or "Show your reasoning" to your prompt. This forces the model to break down the problem, which usually improves accuracy -- similar to how showing your work in a derivation catches errors.

"I need to design a bandpass filter for my optical setup. The requirements are: center wavelength 800nm, bandwidth 10nm, transmission >90%. Think step by step about the design options, trade-offs, and recommend a specific approach."

ChatGPT Plus can process uploaded files -- CSV data, PDF papers, images, code files. Click the attachment icon and upload directly.

"I have uploaded a CSV file with my experimental data. The columns are wavelength (nm) and absorbance (a.u.). Plot the spectrum, identify the main absorption peaks, and fit each peak with a Gaussian."

In ChatGPT Settings > Personalization > Custom Instructions, you can set permanent context that applies to all conversations:

"I am a faculty member in applied physics at KTH. My research focuses on ultrafast spectroscopy and nonlinear optics. When I ask about data analysis, use Python with numpy, scipy, and matplotlib. Prefer scientific rigor over simplification."

This saves you from repeating your background in every conversation.

This is the most important tip. ChatGPT is confident and articulate -- but it can be wrong. Especially with:

  • Specific numerical values and physical constants
  • Paper citations and references (it frequently fabricates these)
  • Complex multi-step calculations
  • Niche domain-specific facts

Rule of thumb: Use AI for speed, use your expertise for quality. AI is your fast first draft -- you are the reviewer.
